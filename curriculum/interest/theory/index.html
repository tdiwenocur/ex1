ALL CLASS DESCRIPTIONS

Theory and History

  • DES 1 — Introduction to Design
  • Introduction to design discipline through readings, writing, visual problem solving, and critical analysis. Topics: design principles and elements, vocabulary, color theory, Gestalt principles, conceptualization strategies. Role of designer and products in contemporary culture including social responsibility and sustainability.
  • DES 40A — Energy, Materials, and Design Over Time
  • Global history of design across time, viewed through the lens of the effects of the creation and discovery of new energy sources, processes and materials on design.
  • DES 40B — Ideologies of Design
  • Introduction to the history and theory of design in particular relation to political, philosophical, cultural, economic, and environmental debates and objectives.
  • DES 40C — Design for Aesthetics and Experience
  • Global historical survey of design's engagement with changing notions of aesthetics and experience. Relates transformations in the theory, production, and reception of all aspects of design (objects, landscapes, architectures, etc.) to larger cultural, social, and political contexts.
  • DES 128 — Studio Practice in Sustainable Design
  • How is biology influencing architecture and design? This advanced undergraduate/graduate seminar examines recent biological theories and howthey are shaping design theory and practice and being used by artists to challenge us to think critically about these technologies and their outcomes. Examples will come from design, architecture, and art; theo­ries include pattern formation, morphogenesis, artificial life, genetic and tissue engineering, evolutionary programming, self-organization and emergence, social insects, biomimicry, and ecological design.
  • DES 143 — History of Fashion
  • Priority to Design majors. Social context, aesthetics, stylistic developments and methods significant in western hemisphere textiles. Emphasis on the Middle East, Europe, and the Americas up to contemporary times.
  • DES 144 — History of Interior Architecture
  • Thematic survey of interior architecture. Emphasis on dwellings in their cultural settings and development of modern interior design theories. Interiors considered in relation to buildings' exteriors, sites, and uses.
  • DES 145 — History of Visual Communication
  • Historical developments of visual communication, concentrating on the technological and aesthetic development of graphic design; origins and manifestations of current issues in visual communication; provide framework for analysis of current and future trends in visual communication.
  • DES 149 — Information Design: Principles and Practice
  • Design principles and visual strategies for effective information display; analysis of contemporary and historical examples of visual representations and visual narratives in science, humanities, and the arts; emergence of digital methods for interactive data presentation.

FAQ:

Why doesn’t the UC Davis Design Department not have focuses/tracks?

With all the fields of design that are quickly emerging, having a few focuses or tracks can be very limiting for design students now. Also, having a more open program allows students to explore different kinds of design without putting off graduation. It allows for a more personally tailored major for everyone in the program.

Why does the major start with a theoretical, foundational class like Design 1?

The UC Davis Design major is loosely based off of the Bauhaus school from the early 20th century. The Design 1 course is meant as a foundational course, introducing the basics of design thinking, design history, and a little bit of design practice. It’s an introduction so that people who might be interested in design can see if it’s something they want to pursue.


What is the purpose of Curriculum Navigator?

The curriculum navigator is designed to help students, new or otherwise, figure out what kinds of classes they might be interested in taking based on their design interests and tools or skills they want to cultivate.
